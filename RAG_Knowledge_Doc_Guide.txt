RAG Knowledge Document Guide for Overseed AI Assistant
=======================================================


PART 1: What Info to Include in Knowledge Documents
----------------------------------------------------

### Platform-Specific (must have)
- How-to guides: How to create a campaign, apply to campaigns, set up a profile,
  connect social accounts, verify identity
- Feature explanations: What each dashboard section does, what FREE vs PRO includes
- Policies: Payment terms, content guidelines, approval process, dispute resolution
- FAQ: Common questions your support team already gets repeatedly

### Business Knowledge
- Global expansion tips: Market entry strategies by region (US, EU, Asia, etc.),
  cultural considerations, local platform preferences (TikTok vs Douyin, Instagram
  vs Xiaohongshu)
- Creator collaboration best practices: How to write a good brief, typical compensation
  ranges by follower count/platform, do's and don'ts
- Industry benchmarks: Average engagement rates, campaign ROI expectations, content
  formats that perform well

### Format Tips
- Write in Q&A style when possible — this matches how users ask questions, improving
  retrieval accuracy
- Keep each document focused on one topic — don't combine unrelated info in a single doc
- Use clear, simple language — match how users would phrase their questions
- Include keywords users are likely to search for

### What NOT to Include
- Sensitive internal data (financial details, internal strategies)
- Information that changes frequently (unless you plan to update it regularly)
- Generic marketing copy — it won't help answer real questions

Start with your top 20 most common support questions — that alone will cover the
majority of what users ask. You can always add more documents later as you see what
users are asking that the assistant can't answer well.


PART 2: Recommended Format for Knowledge Documents
----------------------------------------------------

Use one document per topic, structured like this:


### Q&A Format (best for FAQs and how-to guides)

    TITLE: How to Create a Campaign
    CATEGORY: platform_guide
    TAGS: campaign, create, setup, brand

    Q: How do I create a new campaign on Overseed?
    A: Go to your Brand Dashboard and click "Campaigns" in the sidebar. Then click
    "New Campaign" and fill in the following:
    - Campaign name
    - Description and goals
    - Target platforms (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, etc.)
    - Budget and compensation type
    - Timeline and deadlines
    - Target regions/markets

    Once everything is filled in, click "Publish" to make it visible to creators.

    Q: Can I edit a campaign after publishing?
    A: Yes. Go to Campaigns > click on your campaign > Edit. You can update any
    field. Note that creators who already applied will be notified of changes.

    Q: How do I close a campaign?
    A: Go to your campaign page and click "Close Campaign". This stops new
    applications but doesn't affect existing ones.


### Informational Format (best for policies, pricing, guides)

    TITLE: Overseed Pricing Plans
    CATEGORY: faq
    TAGS: pricing, free, pro, subscription

    Overseed offers two plans:

    FREE Plan:
    - Create up to 3 campaigns
    - Basic analytics
    - Access to creator marketplace
    - Email support

    PRO Plan ($29/month):
    - Unlimited campaigns
    - Advanced analytics and reporting
    - AI Assistant access
    - Priority support
    - Market insights by region
    - Creator recommendation engine


### Why This Format Works Well for RAG

- Q&A pairs closely match how users ask questions, so the embeddings will have
  higher similarity scores during retrieval
- One topic per document keeps chunks focused — when the system retrieves a chunk,
  it gets relevant context, not a mix of unrelated info
- TITLE/CATEGORY/TAGS at the top help with organization in the admin UI and can
  be used for filtering
- Short, direct answers — the AI can use these as-is or rephrase naturally
- Multiple related Q&As in one doc — they'll be chunked together, giving the AI
  fuller context on the topic
- Short paragraphs work best — the chunker splits on double newlines, so each
  section naturally becomes its own retrievable chunk
